How to use the QADT Dimensional Analysis Tool


This tool is provided to evaluate equations using quantity kinds in the QUDT vocabulary. You create an equation’s left and right sides using quantity kinds (text input / drop down values) and the application provides the dimension vectors for the selected items as well as evaluation whether the left and right sides of the equation are balanced.

Usage

  1. You can start typing the name of a desired Quantity Kind and those matching what you the will show up in the Quantity Kind dropdown list. Or you can select a desired Quantity Kind from the list directly. The list is populated by executing a SPARQL query on the QUDT triple store, satisfying: ?quantitykind a qudt:QuantityKind (plus a filter against the text you have typed). Once a quantity kind is selected, this will narrow the types of starting and target units available to those that are applicable to the quantity kind. That is, they satisfy the following property in the QUDT triple store: qudt:applicableUnit ?unit. 
  2. The left and right side quantity kind dropdowns are identical. 
  3. When you find a desirable quantity kind (e.g, Force) you will select the ‘Add Quantity’ button. This will add the quantity into the equation list, get its dimension vector from the vocabulary, get its dimension vector exponents, and aggregate those exponents into an ‘effective’ dimension vector for the side of the equation you are working on. 
  4. When you have performed the operations above for both sides of the equation you will be told whether the equation is balanced or not (i.e., the units match).
